Welcome to my personal website. I am a PhD candidate at the Anton Pannekoek Astronomical Institute, University of Amsterdam and the Department of Earth Sciences, VU University Amsterdam. I am working with Prof. Carsten Dominik in the Exoplanets and Disks group and Prof. Wim van Westrenen in the Planetary Science group. My current research interest lies in the study of the interior of rocky extra-solar planets.

Research

The extra-solar planets, or exoplanets, are the planets in orbit around stars other than our sun. There are more than 1000 billion exoplanets in our galaxy alone. Some of these exoplanets are like our Earth and it is not so strange to think that at least a few of them can harbour alien life! As a scientist, I am interested in investigating what these exopanets are made up of.

I am working in the high-pressure laboratory of VU University to study the interior structure and composition of rocky exoplanets. Currently, I am studying the minerals and their phases present in the interior of carbon-rich rocky exoplanets by performing high-pressure-temperature laboratory experiments. I have also developed models to theoretically compute the interior structure of rocky exoplanets and their mass-radius relations.
